Recurring Transactions on the Ethereum Blockchain

  1. Sign in to your Ethereum Account
  2. Deploy your Everchain Wallet
  3. Create your first Recurring Transaction (RTx)

Blockchains provide unstoppable transactions but also have an inherent problem: nothing happens automatically. Someone in the real world must create and send a transaction to interact with the blockchain. The Ethereum Alarm Clock solves this problem by creating a one time use alarm that will financially incentivize someone else to safely trigger a transaction on your behalf at a future time.

keyboard_arrow_right keyboard_arrow_down

Everchain RTx takes this one step further with recurring transaction smart contracts. An RTx will automatically execute an on-chain task at a future time and safely refunds itself for the next execution. A Recurring Transaction can interact with any smart contract in the Ethereum Network and as long as the supplying wallet has enough funds to pay for execution, the recurring transactions will continue to function.

Transaction Costs

RTx Cost = RTx Gas Used * Current Gas Price * Safety Multiplier

The current gas price is set by the Bancor network. The Bancor Network needs a maximum gas price to prevent front running but an RTx repurposes this value as the best current gas price.

The Safety Multiplier is a temporary measure to ensure in the event of a drastic price drop or spike in gas costs, the alarm will still have enough funds to execute. In the future, a new mechanism can be created that funds alarms at the time of execution and removes the need for an oracle. In addition, the new mechanism will also enable paying in any desired ERC20 token such as Dai.
